Why DKI got funded — anatomy of a $300K pitch.

The slide that turned a side project into a board-approved platform. What I would change. What I would never change.

Written by
Santosh B. Pathak · Mumbai

In late 2024 I pitched a platform called Desh Ka Insurance to the Dai-ichi Life Innovation Fund. A Progressive Web App enabling rural POS agents to onboard, train, and sell microinsurance across India. They funded it — $100K PoC, $200K seed, plus a blockchain-verified DINO credential from Dai-ichi Life Holdings. Here's how the pitch worked.

The problem statement

Microinsurance is the right product for 60Cr+ rural Indian adults. But distribution is broken — POS agents have no onboarding, no training tools, no digital surface. So insurers default to "not our market." The opportunity is enormous; the unit economics, untested.

The pitch in one slide

  • Market: 60Cr+ rural adults, microinsurance category growing 18% YoY.
  • Problem: POS agents in tier-3/4 cities can't sell what they can't access.
  • Solution: A PWA — works on a basic Android phone, low data — for onboarding, training, and selling.
  • Why us: SUD Life has the parent network (Bank of India + Union Bank). We can ship pilots through real branches.
  • Ask: $300K — $100K PoC, $200K seed. 12-month milestones. Quarterly board reviews.
The pitch worked because it wasn't a pitch. It was a business case the board could underwrite.

What made it land

Tied to a balance sheet. Innovation that doesn't end in a revenue line gets sliced from budget in Q4. DKI had revenue projections, agent-onboarding targets, and a unit economics model attached on slide 7.

Used existing distribution. A new platform with a new distribution channel is two startups stitched together. DKI used Bank of India and Union Bank branches as launch sites. One unknown, not two.

Compliance-pre-cleared. Before pitching, I had three informal calls with our IRDAI team to confirm microinsurance distribution via PWA was viable. The board never had to ask the question.

What I'd change

I'd have pitched six months earlier. I was over-protecting the foundation work and under-pitching the platform. The appetite existed; I confused "cautious" with "unavailable".

What I'd never change

Treating the Innovation Fund like a venture capital firm. Milestones. Quarterly updates. Honest reporting on what shifted. Internal funds get the same discipline as external ones; otherwise they atrophy.

If you're putting together an innovation pitch inside a BFSI org and want a second set of eyes — happy to read it.
