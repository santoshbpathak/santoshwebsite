The in-house AI studio playbook for regulated industries.

Generative AI that clears IRDAI in one pass. Tooling, SOPs, model selection — and the four governance principles you can't skip.

Written by
Santosh B. Pathak · Mumbai

The shiny version of generative AI in marketing — "type a prompt, ship a campaign" — falls apart on contact with any regulated category. Insurance, banking, healthcare, pharma. The work isn't generating content. It's generating content a compliance team will sign off on without a second pass.

I'm building one of these systems for SUD Life right now. Here are the architectural decisions that survived first contact with IRDAI.

What an AI studio actually is

Not a tool. A system — model layer, prompt layer, workflow layer, governance layer. Most teams buy a tool, run two months of experiments, declare the AI strategy "done", and quietly fold it back into a content calendar. That's the failure mode.

A studio looks more like this:

  • Model layer: 2–3 frontier models selected for task fit — long-form reasoning, structured output, image generation.
  • Prompt layer: versioned, tested prompt templates for every recurring content type.
  • Workflow layer: human-in-the-loop checkpoints. Editorial first, compliance second, brand third.
  • Governance layer: red lines that never reach a human reviewer — they're filtered at generation.
The goal isn't "AI in the loop". The goal is content that's faster, cheaper, and more compliant than the agency baseline. Anything less is theatre.

Four governance principles you can't skip

One — Compliance is a system, not a review step. If your compliance team is reading every output, you've built a slower agency. Compliance has to be encoded — restricted vocabulary, mandatory disclosures, prohibited claims — at the prompt layer.

Two — Brand voice gets a training set. A generic frontier model writes like everyone's marketing. We feed our editorial style guide, our last 200 approved pieces, and three "voice anchors" into the system prompt. Output recognisably ours from the first draft.

Three — Auditability is non-negotiable. Every output stores its prompt, model version, and reviewer chain. If IRDAI asks how a piece of content was produced, the answer takes five minutes, not five weeks.

Four — Humans stay in the loop, but only at the meaningful steps. Editorial taste, compliance edge cases, brand judgement. Not grammar, not formatting, not first drafts.

What we're targeting

  • 3x content output vs. the agency baseline by FY27.
  • 2x Share of Voice in the Indian life insurance category.
  • 100% compliance retention — zero regressions on IRDAI audit.
  • ~60% cost reduction on content production at scale.

Those are bets, not promises. We will report against them publicly when FY27 closes.

The thing nobody tells you

The hardest part of building an AI studio isn't technical. It's persuading your own organisation that "human-reviewed AI output" is not lower quality than "human-only output". The political work — getting brand, compliance, and editorial to trust the system — takes longer than building it.
